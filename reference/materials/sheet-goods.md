# Sheet Goods Reference

Standard sheet size: 4' x 8' (48" x 96")

## Plywood

### Hardwood Plywood (Cabinet Grade)
| Type | Common Thicknesses | Face/Back Grade | Primary Use |
|------|-------------------|-----------------|-------------|
| Birch | 1/4", 1/2", 3/4" | A/B or B/B | Paint-grade cabinets, shelving |
| Maple | 1/4", 1/2", 3/4" | A/B | Paint-grade, very smooth surface |
| Oak (Red) | 1/4", 1/2", 3/4" | A/B | Stain-grade cabinets |
| Oak (White) | 1/4", 1/2", 3/4" | A/B | Stain-grade, more moisture-resistant |
| Walnut | 1/4", 1/2", 3/4" | A/B | Premium stain-grade |
| Cherry | 1/4", 1/2", 3/4" | A/B | Premium stain-grade |

**Actual thicknesses** (plywood is often undersized):
- 1/4" nominal = ~0.210" - 0.230" actual
- 1/2" nominal = ~0.460" - 0.480" actual
- 3/4" nominal = ~0.710" - 0.730" actual

### Softwood Plywood
| Type | Common Thicknesses | Grade | Primary Use |
|------|-------------------|-------|-------------|
| CDX | 1/2", 3/4" | C/D Exposure 1 | Substrates, hidden structural |
| Sanded Pine | 1/4", 1/2", 3/4" | B/C | Utility shelving, cabinet backs |

### Specialty Plywood
| Type | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Baltic Birch | True 1/2" and 3/4" thickness, void-free core, 5'x5' sheets |
| Marine Grade | Waterproof glue, no voids, expensive |
| Bending Plywood | 1/4" or thinner, for curved forms |

## MDF (Medium Density Fiberboard)
| Thickness | Primary Use | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| 1/4" | Cabinet backs, drawer bottoms | Lighter than plywood |
| 1/2" | Shelving, panels | |
| 3/4" | Doors, face frames (paint only) | Machines beautifully, heavy |

**MDF Notes:**
- Takes paint exceptionally well (smoother than plywood)
- Cannot be stained (no grain)
- Heavy — 3/4" sheet weighs ~90 lbs
- Swells when exposed to water — not for bathrooms without sealing all edges
- Dust is very fine — always wear a respirator

## Melamine / Particle Board
| Thickness | Primary Use | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| 3/4" | Cabinet interiors, closet systems | Pre-finished, white or colors |
| 5/8" | Shelving | |

**Melamine Notes:**
- Pre-finished = no painting/staining needed
- Edges need iron-on edge banding
- Screws don't hold as well as in plywood — use confirmats or pocket screws with care
- Chips easily during cutting — use fine-tooth blade, cut good face up on table saw

## Edge Banding
| Type | Width | Application | Notes |
|------|-------|-------------|-------|
| Iron-on (PVC) | 3/4", 1-1/2", 2" | Heat with iron, trim flush | Most common |
| Peel-and-stick | 3/4", 1-1/2" | Peel backing, press on | Quicker, less durable |
| Hardwood strip | 1/4" x 3/4" | Glue and clamp | Best appearance for stain-grade |

## Cutting Tips for Sheet Goods
- **Circular saw + Accu-Cut:** Cut good face DOWN (blade enters from bottom)
- **Table saw:** Cut good face UP
- **Support the sheet:** Use sawhorses or foam board underneath to prevent binding
- **Score first:** For melamine, score the cut line with a utility knife to prevent chipping
- **Blade:** Use 40T+ carbide blade with alternate top bevel (ATB) grind for clean cuts
