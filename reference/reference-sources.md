# Cabinetry, Cabinet Building & Woodworking Resource Guide

---

## Section 1 — Forums & Communities

These are your go-to places for asking questions, getting real-world build advice, and searching years of archived knowledge.

**WOODWEB Cabinetmaking Forum** — `woodweb.com/cgi-bin/forums/cabinetmaking.pl`
WOODWEB is a professional industrial woodworking site, covering cabinetmaking topics at a trade level. Covers sheet goods, CNC, finishing, hardware, and shop workflow at a professional level. Skews toward shop owners and serious craftspeople, but the archive is invaluable for anyone building at a high level.

**Sawmill Creek Woodworking Community** — `sawmillcreek.org`
Sawmill Creek is an active woodworking community where members discuss everything from building kitchen cabinets and sheet goods to skill-building projects like closet organizers. One of the best all-around woodworking forums online, with thousands of threads on kitchen cabinets, built-ins, entertainment centers, jigs, and finishing. Excellent search archive going back years.

**LumberJocks Woodworking Forum** — `lumberjocks.com`
LumberJocks is a forum community dedicated to professional woodworkers and enthusiasts, covering shop safety, carpentry, lumber, finishing, tools, machinery, woodworking styles, reviews, accessories, and classifieds. Great project showcase alongside active forum discussion. Their jigs section is particularly worth searching.

**DoItYourself.com — Carpentry, Cabinetry & Interior Woodworking** — `doityourself.com`
A massive general DIY community with a dedicated cabinetry subforum. Building custom cabinetry seems like a very hard task at first, but if you take one step at a time you will be surprised at how quickly it goes — the community recommends becoming familiar with the techniques by reading books on woodworking and cabinet making before jumping in. Very search-friendly for homeowners tackling their first kitchen or closet build.

**DIYChatroom.com** — `diychatroom.com`
Broad home improvement community, good for installation questions, material selection, and troubleshooting mid-build. Active carpentry and cabinet threads with practical, field-tested answers.

**Router Forums** — `routerforums.com`
A forum community dedicated to router and woodworking professionals and enthusiasts, covering routing and routers, shop safety, finishing, woodworking related topics, styles, tools, scales, reviews, accessories, classifieds, and more. Essential for cabinet door making, dado routing, and router table jig setups.

**Fine Woodworking Forum** — `finewoodworking.com/forum`
The companion forum to the magazine. High-quality technical discussion on joinery, cabinet construction, furniture making, and tools. Archive of detailed threads going back decades.

**Cabinet Makers Association (CMA)** — `cabinetmakers.org/forums`
A major advantage of CMA membership is the cooperative nature of members providing exclusive peer-to-peer networking, where members share their knowledge, expertise, and abilities — giving you access to the accumulated knowledge and experience of hundreds of woodworking business owners. Membership-based, worth knowing about if you ever go pro.

## Section 2 — Cabinetry Websites, Plans & Blogs

**Fine Woodworking** — `finewoodworking.com`
Fine Woodworking provides expert advice on woodworking and furniture making, with thousands of how-to videos, step-by-step articles, project plans, photo galleries, tool reviews, and blogs. The gold standard. Deep library of cabinet-specific content, video workshops, and digital plans. Some content is paywalled but the free archive is extensive.

**Popular Woodworking** — `popularwoodworking.com`
Popular Woodworking provides resources, projects, how-to articles, and artist features from contributors including Roy Underhill, Christopher Schwarz, Mary May, Bob Flexner, and others. Their dedicated Jigs & Fixtures section is one of the most complete written resources for shop-made jig construction anywhere online.

**Woodshop Diaries** — `woodshopdiaries.com`
Excellent DIY-focused site with detailed plans and build logs for entertainment centers, closet systems, cabinet doors, and drawer builds. Their entertainment center plans walk through building cabinet boxes from plywood with pocket holes, face frames, crown molding, and concealed hinges, with a center opening sized for large TVs. Free downloadable plans with clear photography.

**Rogue Engineer** — `rogueengineer.com`
Rogue Engineer was created so that average folks and weekend warriors with a few tools could build awesome looking furniture for next to nothing — an aerospace engineer using his expertise in mechanical design to create simple DIY furniture plans that eliminate mistakes and cut down on project time. Their built-in entertainment center and bookcase plans are particularly well-engineered.

**Woodworkers Guild of America (WWGOA)** — `wwgoa.com`
WWGOA has been creating instructional video content for woodworkers since 2008, including cabinet-specific content like shop-made jigs for dado sizing, shelf pin drilling, and circle cutting. Mix of free and premium video content. Their cabinetmaking jig content is excellent.

**Ana White** — `ana-white.com`
Ana White helps people improve their homes with basic tools on limited budgets, with a decade of designing and building furniture plans shared freely on her website. Huge library of Shaker-style and farmhouse cabinet, bookshelf, and storage plans — all free.

**Jay's Custom Creations** — `jayscustomcreations.com`
Jay Bates' blog covering shop projects, built-ins, furniture, and custom woodworking. Well-documented builds with companion YouTube videos. Strong on shop fixtures and face-frame cabinet builds.

**Kreg Tool Learning Center** — `learn.kregtool.com`
Kreg's own education hub with dozens of free cabinet, closet, and furniture plans built around their pocket-hole system. Includes face-frame cabinet guides and jig tutorials. Their closet section alone has 25+ plans.

**THISisCarpentry (TiC)** — `thisiscarpentry.com`
THISisCarpentry is an interactive, multimedia e-magazine written by carpenters, for carpenters, offering detailed, well-illustrated articles about construction techniques, new tools of the trade, improving carpentry skills, and much more. High signal-to-noise ratio for trade-level content.

**Woodworking Network — Cabinets** — `woodworkingnetwork.com/cabinets`
Trade publication covering the custom cabinet and millwork industry. Good for understanding the professional side — finishing technology, CNC, shop equipment, and manufacturing trends.

**Her Tool Belt** — `hertoolbelt.com`
DIY project blog with solid entertainment center and built-in plans. Her tutorial on building bookshelves and entertainment center components explains face frame joinery using biscuit cutters, doweling jigs, and pocket holes, with notes on when each method is appropriate. Good source for Pottery Barn-inspired builds.

**WoodWorkWeb** — `woodworkweb.com`
Making jigs and uncovering woodworking tips seems to have become second nature at WoodWorkWeb, covering everything from thin strip ripping jigs and dado sizing jigs to veneer jigs and joinery planning jigs. Video-heavy with a massive jig library.

**Rockler — Learn Section** — `rockler.com/learn`
Free plans, how-to articles, and video guides. Particularly strong for hardware installation tutorials — drawer slides, soft-close hinges, shelf pin drilling, and more. Their closet organizer plan using French cleats is excellent.

## Section 3 — Built-Ins: Entertainment Centers, Bookshelves & Closets

**The Sagulator** — `woodbin.com/calcs/sagulator`
Not a blog — a single essential calculator. Enter shelf material, thickness, span, and load to calculate expected sag before you build. Referenced constantly on Sawmill Creek. Bookmark it for every shelving project.

For **entertainment centers**, the best plan sources are:
- **Woodshop Diaries** — full plans including cabinet doors, face frames, and crown molding
- **Rogue Engineer** — 15-foot built-in plan with lower cabinets and flanking bookcases
- **Ana White** — dozens of beginner-friendly entertainment center and bookcase plans
- **Her Tool Belt** — Pottery Barn-inspired media center builds

For **closet systems**, start with:
- **Kreg Tool** (`learn.kregtool.com/projects-plans/closet`) — 25+ plans for modular closet towers, hanging sections, drawers, shoe cubbies
- **Rockler** (`rockler.com/learn/diy-closet-organizer-plan`) — French cleat closet system where every component hangs at any height and rearranges freely
- **Woodshop Diaries** — closet system with large drawers, shoe cubbies, and pull-out belt rack

Sawmill Creek members recommend using plywood for closet carcasses, building on a leveled platform, cutting the baseboard or building above it, and joining with a combination of wood glue and screws — with pocket holes or dados as the preferred joinery method.

For **entertainment center AV considerations**, Sawmill Creek members recommend putting AV components on slide-out shelves rather than fixed ones, using infrared repeaters to control equipment behind cabinet doors, and planning for ventilation since even a cable box generates significant heat.

## Section 4 — Woodworking Education: Techniques, Jigs & Skill Building

**The Wood Whisperer** — `thewoodwhisperer.com`
Marc Spagnuolo's site is widely regarded as one of the best woodworking education resources online. Detailed project builds, joinery deep-dives, tool reviews, and finishing content. Recommended constantly across all the forums above.

**Paul Sellers Blog** — `paulsellers.com`
Hand tool woodworking education from a master craftsman. His blog and companion site **Common Woodworking** (`commonwoodworking.com`) are among the best beginner resources available, emphasizing accessible tools and fundamental joinery.

**Lost Art Press** — `lostartpress.com`
Publisher and blog focused on preserving traditional woodworking knowledge. Deep, well-researched articles on hand tool techniques, historical methods, and workbench design. Christopher Schwarz is the primary author.

**The Renaissance Woodworker** — `renaissancewoodworker.com`
Shannon Rogers' site focused on hand tool woodworking education. Good for learning sharpening, traditional joinery, and hand tool fundamentals that complement power-tool cabinet work.

**Stumpy Nubs Woodworking Journal** — `stumpynubs.com`
Creative, entertaining power tool woodworking content. Known for budget-friendly jig solutions and practical shop advice. Their YouTube channel is consistently one of the highest recommended by Sawmill Creek members for jig ideas.

**Christofix** — `christofix.com`
Christofix covers must-have beginner woodworking jigs, recommending hardwood plywood over MDF for jig construction because MDF is less stable, warps faster due to moisture, and wears out sooner — reducing accuracy over time. Free plans for five essential starter jigs.

**DIY Montreal** — `diymontreal.com`
Their jig tutorials cover thin strip ripping jigs, circular saw guides, vertical cutting jigs, dado sizing jigs, and keyhole router jigs — all built from scrap material in minutes. Practical, no-frills jig building.

**Popular Woodworking — Jigs & Fixtures** — `popularwoodworking.com/jigs-and-fixtures-projects`
Their jig library covers drawer slide installation jigs, box joint jigs, crosscut sleds, router table accessories, dovetail jigs, mortising fences, and cabinet hardware jigs — one of the most complete written jig resources available.

**Wood and Shop** — `woodandshop.com`
Traditional woodworking blog with strong beginner resources, video content, and project walkthroughs. Good for hand tool fundamentals.

**Blacktail Studio Blog** — `blacktailstudio.com`
Blacktail Studio creates free woodworking tutorials and shares projects, tips, and techniques through how-to videos and blogging, covering both beginner and experienced woodworkers. More furniture-focused but strong on shop process and finishing.

**WoodNet Links Directory** — `woodnet.net/links`
An older but still useful curated directory of woodworking sites, tool vendors, plan sources, and clubs. Good for finding niche suppliers like specialty blade sharpening services and custom router bit cutters.

## Section 5 — Hardware & Supply Stores Worth Knowing

**Rockler Woodworking & Hardware** — `rockler.com`
Rockler caters to everyone from beginners setting up their first garage workshop to experienced professionals building fine furniture and cabinetry, offering specialized woodworking products not found in big-box stores, well-designed jigs and accessories, hardware tailored for furniture and cabinetry, and educational content.

**Woodcraft** — `woodcraft.com`
Woodcraft carries trusted brands like HIGHPOINT and WoodRiver for cabinet hardware, including vintage hardware for restorations and modern hardware for new builds. Physical stores nationwide, good for hand tools and specialty hardwoods.

**Lee Valley Tools** — `leevalley.com`
The best source for Veritas hand tools, precision drill guide bushings, specialty shelf pin hardware, and jig-making components. Their drill guide bushings are referenced across multiple cabinet forums for shelf-pin drilling jigs.

**Woodworker's Source** — `woodworkerssource.com/blog`
Arizona-based specialty lumber and hardwood supplier (relevant to you in the East Valley). Good blog covering wood species selection, plywood grading, and grain matching for cabinet faces.

## Section 6 — YouTube Channels

The forums above converge on a consistent short list of YouTube channels most recommended for cabinet and woodworking education:

Forum members consistently recommend The Wood Whisperer, Steve Ramsey (Woodworking for Mere Mortals), and Stumpy Nubs as starting points, alongside channels from Popular Woodworking, Woodcraft, and Rockler for supplemental content.

The full list worth subscribing to, based on cross-referencing Sawmill Creek threads:

- **The Wood Whisperer** (Marc Spagnuolo) — high-quality technique and project builds
- **Paul Sellers** — hand tool mastery and fundamental joinery
- **Jay Bates** (Jay's Custom Creations) — shop projects and furniture builds
- **Steve Ramsey** (Woodworking for Mere Mortals) — beginner-friendly weekend projects
- **Stumpy Nubs** — clever jig solutions and shop tips on a budget
- **Fix This Build That** (Brad Rodriguez) — great for drawer hardware and cabinet assembly
- **Woodshop Diaries** — companion videos to the website's plans
- **Crafted Workshop** — modern furniture and storage builds with great production
- **John Heisz (I Build It)** — tool-building, shop jigs, and fixtures from scratch
- **Make Something** (David Picciuto) — fun, creative, educational build content
- **Blacktail Studio** — modern woodworking and shop process content
