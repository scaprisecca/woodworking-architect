# Finishing Reference

## Paint Finish (Most Common for Client Work)

### Process
1. **Sand** to 150-180 grit (remove mill marks, smooth surface)
2. **Fill** any holes, gaps, or imperfections with wood filler; sand smooth
3. **Prime** with high-quality primer (BIN Shellac or quality latex primer)
   - Sand primer lightly with 220 grit after it dries
4. **First coat** of paint — light, even coat
   - Sand lightly with 220 grit between coats
5. **Second coat** of paint — final coat, even coverage
6. **Optional third coat** for high-wear surfaces

### Recommended Products
| Product | Use | Notes |
|---------|-----|-------|
| BIN Shellac Primer | Best adhesion, stain blocking | Strong odor, use ventilation |
| Benjamin Moore Advance | Cabinet paint (alkyd/latex hybrid) | Self-leveling, durable, long dry time |
| Sherwin-Williams Emerald Urethane | Cabinet paint | Excellent durability |
| General Finishes Milk Paint | Cabinet paint | Good for spray or brush |

### Tips
- **Spray vs brush:** Spray gives the best factory-like finish. If brushing, use a quality angled brush and foam roller
- **Thin coats:** Multiple thin coats > one thick coat
- **Temperature:** Paint in 60°-80°F, 40-60% humidity
- **Cure time:** Paint is dry to touch in hours but takes 2-4 weeks to fully cure. Warn clients not to scrub for a month
- **Door removal:** Always remove doors and hardware before painting. Finish both sides equally to prevent warping

## Stain + Clear Coat

### Process
1. **Sand** to 180-220 grit
2. **Pre-stain conditioner** (for blotch-prone species: cherry, maple, birch, pine)
3. **Stain** — apply with rag or brush, wipe off excess per manufacturer instructions
4. **Seal** with 1 coat of sanding sealer or thinned clear coat
5. **Sand** lightly with 320 grit
6. **Clear coat** — 2-3 coats, sanding 320 grit between coats

### Clear Coat Options
| Product | Durability | Appearance | Dry Time | Notes |
|---------|-----------|------------|----------|-------|
| Water-based polyurethane | Good | Clear, non-yellowing | 2-4 hrs | Easy cleanup, low odor |
| Oil-based polyurethane | Excellent | Amber warmth, yellows over time | 6-12 hrs | More durable, longer dry |
| Lacquer | Excellent | Crystal clear | 30 min | Best finish, requires spray equipment + ventilation |
| Conversion varnish | Professional | Crystal clear | 30 min | Most durable, requires catalyzed mixing |

### Tips
- **Test stain color** on scrap of the SAME species before committing
- **Grain direction:** Always sand with the grain
- **End grain:** Seal end grain with thinned shellac before staining to prevent blotchy absorption

## Natural / Oil Finish

### Options
| Product | Use | Notes |
|---------|-----|-------|
| Danish oil | Furniture, shelving | Easy to apply, satin finish |
| Tung oil | Durable natural finish | Multiple coats needed, long cure |
| Rubio Monocoat | Premium one-coat oil | Expensive, beautiful result |
| Mineral oil | Cutting boards, food contact | Must be reapplied regularly |

## Sanding Schedule
| Grit | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| 80 | Heavy material removal, shaping only |
| 120 | Initial smoothing, removing mill marks |
| 150 | Standard pre-finish sanding |
| 180 | Fine pre-finish sanding |
| 220 | Between coats, final pre-stain |
| 320 | Between clear coats |
| 400+ | Final polish (for lacquer/high-gloss) |

**Rule:** Never skip more than one grit level. 120 → 180 → 220, not 120 → 220.
